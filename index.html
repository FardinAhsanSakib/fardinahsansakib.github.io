Fardin Ahsan Sakib

PhD Candidate, Computer Science
George Mason University

Graduate Research Assistant @ George Mason University AI Research Intern @ Comcast

I am a doctoral researcher in the Department of Computer Science at George Mason University and a member of the MasonNLP Research Group, working to make large language models trustworthy enough for high-stakes use. I develop interpretability and post-training methods that diagnose and repair reliability and bias failures in LLMs — including inference-time corrections that cut biased predictions by up to 78% without retraining — with applications in clinical NLP and healthcare, published at venues such as ACL and EMNLP. I am now extending this work to AI agents: AI safety in multi-agent systems, together with the capabilities at their core — agentic memory and rigorous agent evaluation. Through research internships at Comcast, Fujitsu Research, and Amazon, I bring the same rigor to applied agentic AI, from coding agents to large-scale, tool-calling evaluation.
